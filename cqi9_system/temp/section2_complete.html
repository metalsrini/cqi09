Section 2 - Shop Floor Process Control
Please describe Objective Evidence for each Requirement
2.1 Are all furnaces or other treatment sources used for heat treating controlled?
Process control parameters shall be defined and fully documented.
Requirements Objective Evidence Assessment
Parameters shall be defined and documented in the process control plan.
Process parameters shall be monitored at a frequency defined by the organization to demonstrate compliance with customer requirements and effective equipment operation.
Parameters to be monitored may include but are not limited to, load configuration, temperature, atmosphere, quench medium, flow rates, carbon potential, pressure, etc.
Comments:
2.2 Are there requirements related to process parameter monitoring?
Process parameters for heat treat equipment shall be monitored to ensure control of the process.
Requirements Objective Evidence Assessment
Verification of process parameters for key processes shall be monitored and documented utilizing appropriate data recording devices, such as strip charts, data acquisition systems, or operator data sheets. Recording devices and data acquisition systems, when used, shall be calibrated.
Process monitoring shall include actions to be taken when out of control conditions are identified.
Comments:
2.3 Does the organization have a system to identify and trace loads?
Loads must be identified and traced throughout all processing steps.
Requirements Objective Evidence Assessment
The organization shall identify all heat treat loads through all process steps.
The organization shall establish and maintain documented procedures for identification and traceability of each heat treat load throughout all process steps.
Comments:
2.4 Are heat treated products validated?
Heat treat processes shall be validated and monitored to ensure specified property requirements are achieved.
Requirements Objective Evidence Assessment
The organization shall have a method for validating that the heat treat process has met all customer requirements.
When periodic testing is required by customer, the organization shall have the ability to perform that testing or utilize an accredited lab.
Comments:
2.5 Are the test and inspection methods verified?
Test and inspection methods shall be verified for accuracy and reliability.
Requirements Objective Evidence Assessment
In-house test methods shall be verified when available industry standards are in use. When industry standards are not available, alternative verification methods shall be used.
The organization shall correlate test results with accredited sources when industry standards are not available.
